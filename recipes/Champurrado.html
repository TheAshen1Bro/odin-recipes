Champurrado

Description

Champurrado is a Mexican recipe I and many of my famiy members would have for breakfast growing up. You usually serve it really hot in a mug such as the one pictured above. It goes great with pastries such as bollilo, conchas, cuernitos. It also goes great with other Mexican foods such as tamales.

Champurrado is fairly simple to make and shouldn't take more than 15 minutes. It's the chocolate version of what is known as Atole. In my opinion it is better, but you can enjoy both by just taking the chocolate out of the equation. Hope you enjoy drinking it as much as I did growing up.

Ingredients

  • 2 Abuelita Mexican chocolate tablets
  • 5 cinnamon sticks
  • 1/2 cup of corn starch
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 3 cups of water

Steps

  1. Set stove on medium/high
  2. Heat up 3 cups of water with 5 canela sticks and 2 Abuelita Mexican chocolate tablets.
  3. Mix 1/2 cup of corn starch with 1/2 cup of milk until mixed well.
  4. Pour mix into boiling water and stir every 2 minutes for 10 minutes.
  5. Enjoy!
