Tomato Rice

Description

Tomato rice is another Mexican dish I ate growing up. It compliments lots of other food, especially meats such as chicken, pork, fish, etc. It's fairly simple to make and doesn't take too much time. About 40 minutes maximum.

It's a great starter dish if you're just learning how to cook, especially if you don't get it right the first time. Jasmine rice, which I use, is not expensive so it's good to experiment with the recipe to suit your tastes. It also fills you up fairly decently in case you want something to eat quickly and cost effectively.

Ingredients

  • 1 tomato
  • 1/6 of an onion
  • 1/2 a tooth of garlic
  • 2 spoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 cup of Jasmine rice
  • 1/3 spoon of salt
  • 1/2 spoon of chicken flavor bouillon
  • 1/2 spoon of tomato bouillon with chicken flavor
  • 3 cups of water

Directions

  1. Blend 1 tomato, 1/6 of onion, 1/2 tooth of garlic with 1 cup of water.
  2. Pour 2 spoons of vegetable oil in a pot then pour in 1 cup of Jasmine rice. Stir in pot on medium until rice is golden brown.
  3. Pour blended tomato, onion, garlic mix into pot then pour 2 cups of water.
  4. Add 1/3 spoon of salt, 1/2 spoon of chicken flavor bouillon, and 1/2 spoon of tomato bouillon with chicken flavor.
  5. Stir every 2 minutes for 25 minutes on high until most of the water is evaporated.
  6. Enjoy!
